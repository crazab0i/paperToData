Following the Famenin brucellosis cohort study,
the main aim of the current study was the molecular detection of <italic>Brucella</italic> species (spp.) in sheep and goats from Famenin, Hamedan, Iran.
A total of 23 <italic>Brucella</italic>-seropositive samples (sheep=21 and goats=2), which had been screened from 1,660 animals in the
Famenin cohort study, were used to detect <italic>Brucella</italic>-DNA using
the BCSP31 target gene and IS711 locus. In total, 20 of 23 samples were positive for <italic>Brucella</italic> infection by using specific primers.
Additionally, <italic>Brucella melitensis</italic> (<italic>B. melitensis</italic>) and <italic>Brucella abortus</italic> (<italic>B. abortus</italic>) were
confirmed in 90% (n=18) and 10% (n=2) of positive samples, respectively.
There was no sample with the co-infection of <italic>B. abortus</italic> and <italic>B. melitensis</italic>. In this study, <italic>B. abortus</italic> was isolated from one of the goat samples.
This is the first report on <italic>Brucella</italic> spp. in animals in the region. It was found that <italic>B. melitensis</italic> is the dominant spp. responsible for brucellosis in animals from Famenin.
Molecular techniques are reliable tools to detect <italic>Brucella</italic> infection, especially in cases without serology findings and conclusive results.</p></abstract><kwd-group><kwd> Animal</kwd><kwd><italic>Brucella abortus</italic></kwd><kwd><italic>Brucella melitensis</italic></kwd><kwd> Famenin</kwd><kwd> Molecular detection</kwd></kwd-group></article-meta></front>
