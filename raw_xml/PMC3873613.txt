ANNOUNCEMENT</title><p>Brucellosis remains a major public health concern worldwide and is the most common zoonotic infection (<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B1">1</xref>). The brucellosis eradication programs are based on the monitoring of epizootic processes, vaccination with the recommended live vaccines, or elimination of immunopositive animals (<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B2">2</xref>). Since 1974, the former Soviet republics have officially started to use the live vaccine strain of <italic toggle="yes">Brucella abortus</italic> 82. As a result, the total number of infected farms in Russia fell by 80 times during the period of the successful application of the vaccine. In 2008, less than 70 farms were officially registered as infected (<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>). Success of this strain in vaccination is explained by the combination of a weak agglutination test response with high immunogenicity and comparatively high efficacy against brucellosis. In addition, strain 82 has low residual virulence and is stable during <italic toggle="yes">in vitro</italic> passaging in media and <italic toggle="yes">in vivo</italic> passaging in guinea pigs and cattle, including pregnant cows (<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B3">3</xref>&#8211;<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B5">5</xref>). Due to prolonged intracellular persistence of <italic toggle="yes">Brucella</italic> in the infected lymphoid tissues and probability of abortion in the vaccinated animals, genetic identification of <italic toggle="yes">Brucella</italic> isolates is required (<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="B6">6</xref>). Here, we report the draft genome sequence of <italic toggle="yes">Brucella abortus</italic> strain 82, which may help to elucidate the causes of low residual virulence and to develop a diagnostic approach for the differentiation of vaccine and infectious strains.</p><p>The genomic DNA was isolated from the strain <italic toggle="yes">Brucella abortus</italic> 82, deposited at the National Veterinary Reference Center in Astana, Kazakhstan. The whole-genome shotgun (WGS) sequence data were retrieved with the Ion Torrent sequencing platform, which gave 85-fold coverage. A total of 66 contigs with an <italic toggle="yes">N</italic><sub>50</sub> length of 107,330&#160;bp were assembled using Newbler software v. 2.3 (454 Life Science). The genome was annotated using the NCBI Prokaryotic Genome Annotation Pipeline 2013. The draft whole-genome sequence of the strain <italic toggle="yes">Brucella abortus</italic> 82 has 3,250,206 bases that include 2,880 predicted coding sequences (CDS), 3 rRNA and 49 tRNA genes, and a GC content of 57.2%. Comparison of the <italic toggle="yes">Brucella abortus</italic> 82 genome with those of other vaccine or pathogenic strains may show genetic differences responsible for its phenotypic characteristics and pathogenicity. Further analysis and experimental confirmation will be described in our future work.</p><sec id="h1"><title>Nucleotide sequence accession numbers.</title><p>This whole-genome shotgun project has been deposited at DDBJ/EMBL/GenBank under the accession no. <ext-link xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nuccore?term=AXBQ00000000">AXBQ00000000</ext-link>.
