In this study, an abbreviated <italic>Brucella</italic> AMOS PCR test was evaluated to determine its accuracy in differentiating <italic>Brucella abortus</italic> into three categories: field strains, vaccine strain 19 (S19), and vaccine strain RB51/parent strain 2308 (S2308). Two hundred thirty-one isolates were identified and tested by the conventional biochemical tests and <italic>Brucella</italic> AMOS PCR. This included 120 isolates identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> S19, 9 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> strain RB51, 57 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> biovar 1, 15 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> bv. 2, 1 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> bv. 2 (M antigen dominant), 7 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> bv. 4, and 22 identified as <italic>B. abortus</italic> S2308 and isolated from experimentally infected cattle.
