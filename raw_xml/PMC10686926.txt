      <abstract id="Abs1">
        <sec>
          <title>Background</title>
          <p id="Par1">Brucellosis caused by <italic>B. melitensis</italic> is one of the most important common diseases between humans and livestock. Currently, live attenuated vaccines are used for this disease, which causes many problems, and unfortunately, there is no effective vaccine for human brucellosis. The aim of our research was to design a recombinant vaccine containing potential immunogenic epitopes against <italic>B. melitensis</italic>.</p>
        </sec>
        <sec>
          <title>Methods</title>
          <p id="Par2">In this study, using immunoinformatics approaches, 3 antigens Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 were identified and the amino acid sequence of the selected antigens was determined in NCBI. Signal peptides were predicted by SignaIP-5.0 server. To predict B-cell epitopes from ABCpred and Bcepred servers, to predict MHC-I epitopes from RANKPEP and SYFPEITHI servers, to predict MHC-II epitopes from RANKPEP and MHCPred servers, and to predict CTL epitopes were used from the CTLPred server. Potentially immunogenic final epitopes were joined by flexible linkers. Finally, allergenicity (AllerTOP 2.0 server), antigenicity (Vaxijen server), physicochemical properties (ProtParam server), solubility (Protein-sol server), secondary (PSIPRED and GRO4 servers) and tertiary structure (I-TASSER server), refinement (GalaxyWEB server), validation (ProSA-web, Molprobity, and ERRAT servers), and optimization of the codon sequence (JCat server) of the structure of the multi-epitope vaccine were analyzed.</p>
        </sec>
        <sec>
          <title>Results</title>
          <p id="Par3">The analysis of immunoinformatics tools showed that the designed vaccine has high quality, acceptable physicochemical properties, and can induce humoral and cellular immune responses against <italic>B. melitensis</italic> bacteria. In addition, the high expression level of recombinant antigens in the <italic>E. coli</italic> host was observed through in silico simulation.</p>
        </sec>
        <sec>
          <title>Conclusion</title>
          <p id="Par4">According to the results in silico, the designed vaccine can be a suitable candidate to fight brucellosis and in vitro and in vivo studies are needed to evaluate the research of this study.</p>
        </sec>

    <sec id="Sec1">
      <title>Background</title>
      <p id="Par5"><italic>Brucella</italic> are facultative intracellular and gram-negative coccobacillus bacteria, which causes brucellosis in a variety of animal species and humans. <italic>Brucella melitensis</italic>, <italic>Brucella abortus</italic>, and <italic>Brucella suis</italic> are the maximum malignant species in order of pathogenicity [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR1">1</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par6">Brucellosis is a global zoonotic disease which endemic in plenty countries, including the Latin America, Africa, central Asia, and Middle East [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR2">2</xref>]. This disease not only causes problems for humans, but also causes great damage to livestock and agriculture. Features of the disease with chronic infections with symptoms are such as arthritis, undulating osteomyelitis, and fever in humans, as well as with reduced fertility and abortion in animals [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR3">3</xref>&#x2013;<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR5">5</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par7">Therefore, it is very important to strengthen the diagnosis and treatment of this disease [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR6">6</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR7">7</xref>]. Accordingly, a novel vaccine aimed at stimulating the immune system&#x2019;s response has a high potential for success. But unfortunately, an effective and no side effects vaccine for human brucellosis is not available yet [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR8">8</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR9">9</xref>]. All available animal vaccines are based on live weakened strains of <italic>Brucella</italic> (Rev1, S19, and RB51), which can be potentially effective but are infectious to humans and cause abortion in pregnant animals [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR10">10</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR11">11</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par8">Due to the limitations and disadvantages of live, attenuated vaccines, a new treatment method is needed. Peptide vaccines based on multiple epitopes have advantages compared to live attenuated vaccines, because the production of these novel vaccines is relatively fast and cheap, and they are also safe for the host [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR12">12</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR13">13</xref>]. Research has shown that multi-epitope vaccines containing immune cell epitopes are able to stimulate protective immunity against different diseases [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR14">14</xref>&#x2013;<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR16">16</xref>]. A useful peptide-based vaccine should include T-cell epitopes and B-cell epitopes to stimulate numerous humoral and cellular immunity system response [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR17">17</xref>&#x2013;<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR19">19</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par9">The clue to important brucellosis diagnosis and treatment is to find efficient antigen. Hence, the development of efficient antigens is crucial for improving performance the immune system response of brucellosis multi-epitope vaccines and the sensitivity and specificity of diagnosis and treatment methods [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR20">20</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR21">21</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par10">Nevertheless, the recognition of antigenic epitopes as novel vaccine candidates for designing recombinant vaccines is a costly and time-consuming method. Recently, due to the advances in structure and immunoinformatics vaccinology, the prediction of epitopes for T-lymphocytes and B-lymphocytes helps researchers to identify specific peptides [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR22">22</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR23">23</xref>]. In this work, using previous studies and immunoinformatics tools, three antigens for the new recombinant vaccine against brucellosis including 31&#xA0;kDa outer membrane protein (Omp31), outer membrane protein 25 (Omp25), and outer membrane protein 28 (Omp28) were selected and evaluated. Antigens in the outer membranes of bacteria (OMPs) play an essential role in stimulating the immune system [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR24">24</xref>]. The characteristics of selected antigens are as follows:</p>
      <p id="Par11">Omp31 is a play an essential role in conferring defense against <italic>Brucella</italic> and main membrane protein of <italic>Brucella</italic>. This antigen is a significant virulence factor of <italic>Brucella</italic> and is of high potential as an antigenic protein for vaccination [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR25">25</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par12">Studies have shown that Omp25 protein is highly conserved in different species of <italic>Brucella</italic>. Omp25 is highly immunogenic and leads to an inflammatory response in brucellosis by activating MAPK signaling pathway [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR26">26</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par13">Researchers use suitable adjuvants to induce effective immunity in the vaccine. Adjuvants can increase the immunogenicity of antigens and create effective and long-lasting immune responses [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR27">27</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR28">28</xref>]. Therefore, Omp28 protein was used. Omp28 is considered as an important outer membrane protein of <italic>Brucella</italic> and it has also been reported that Omp28 peptide with CpG oligonucleotide as an adjuvant can induce an immune response, indicating that this protein stimulates the immune system [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR29">29</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par14">Immunoinformatics or computational immunology is the interface between computer science and experimental immunology, which represents the use of computational methods and resources to understand immunological information [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR30">30</xref>]. Therefore, to confirm the availability of the recombinant structure, the physicochemical properties, allergenicity, antigenicity, solubility, secondary, and tertiary structures of the vaccine were analyzed with different immunoinformatics servers. The results showed that the designed recombinant construct can be used to fight <italic>B. melitensis</italic>.</p>
    </sec>
    <sec id="Sec2">
      <title>Methods</title>
      <p id="Par15">Summarized steps of the designing multi-epitope vaccine are presented as flowchart shown in Fig.&#xA0;<xref rid="Fig1" ref-type="fig">1</xref>.<fig id="Fig1"><label>Fig. 1</label><caption><p>Schematic representation of multi-epitope vaccine design against <italic>B. melitensis</italic></p></caption><graphic xlink:href="43141_2023_614_Fig1_HTML" id="MO1"/></fig></p>
      <sec id="Sec3">
        <title>Sequence analysis</title>
        <p id="Par16">The complete amino acid sequences of Omp31 (accession ACS50328.1), Omp25 (accession AEF59022), and Omp28 (accession AEF59021) were retrieved from the National Centre for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) at <ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov">www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov</ext-link> in FASTA format [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR31">31</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec4">
        <title>Prediction of signal peptide</title>
        <p id="Par17">The SignalP-5.0 server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://services.healthtech.dtu.dk/service.php?SignalP-5.0">https://services.healthtech.dtu.dk/service.php?SignalP-5.0</ext-link>) provides a resource for predicting signal peptide cleavage sites in proteins. This server uses convolutional and recurrent (LSTM) neural networks to identify sequence motifs of different lengths [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR32">32</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec5">
        <title>Prediction of B-cell epitopes</title>
        <p id="Par18">The B-cell epitopes of Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 antigens were predicted using ABCpred (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/abcpred/">http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/abcpred/</ext-link>) and Bcepred (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/bcepred/">http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/bcepred/</ext-link>) servers [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR33">33</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR34">34</xref>]. Prediction of B-cell epitopes in ABCpred server using artificial neural network technique [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR35">35</xref>]. Bcepred server to predict B-cell epitopes using physico-chemical properties. The results of Bcepred server are presented in graphic and tabular format. This server predicts epitopes with 58.7% accuracy and 2.38 threshold [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR36">36</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec6">
        <title>Prediction of MHC-I epitopes</title>
        <p id="Par19">The MHC-I epitopes of Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 antigens were predicted by RANKPEP (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://imed.med.ucm.es/Tools/rankpep.html">http://imed.med.ucm.es/Tools/rankpep.html</ext-link>) and SYFPEITHI (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://syfpeithi.de/">http://syfpeithi.de/</ext-link>) server [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR37">37</xref>]. The RANKPEP servers rank all possible epitopes from an input antigen using position-specific scoring matrices (PSSM). MHC-I-restricted epitopes are predicted at a threshold of 2&#x2013;3% of high-scoring peptides [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR38">38</xref>]. SYFPEITHI server uses binding status scoring qualitative prediction methods for T-cell epitope prediction [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR39">39</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec7">
        <title>Prediction of MHC-II epitopes</title>
        <p id="Par20">The MHC-II epitopes of Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 antigens were predicted by MHCPred V.2.0. (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/mhcpred/MHCPred/">http://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/mhcpred/MHCPred/</ext-link>) and RANKPEP (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://imed.med.ucm.es/Tools/rankpep.html">http://imed.med.ucm.es/Tools/rankpep.html</ext-link>) servers. The RANKPEP server ranks all possible epitopes from an input antigen using position-specific scoring matrices (PSSM). MHC-II-restricted epitopes are predicted at a threshold of 4&#x2013;6% of high-scoring peptides [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR38">38</xref>]. MHCPred uses an additive method to predict MHC-II epitopes as well as transporter-associated processing (TAP). The order of epitopes is arranged based on IC50 values. If the IC50 value is higher than 5000, the peptide does not bind to MHC molecules. Therefore, epitopes with lower IC50 values are selected [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR40">40</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec8">
        <title>Prediction of CTL epitopes</title>
        <p id="Par21">The CTL (cytotoxic T lymphocyte) epitopes of Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 antigens was predicted using CTLPred (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/ctlpred/applying">http://crdd.osdd.net/raghava/ctlpred/applying</ext-link>) server. This server predicts CTL epitopes based on artificial neural network (SVM) and support vector machine (ANN). CTLpred server accuracy is 75.8% [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR41">41</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec9">
        <title>Epitope selection and construction of novel vaccine</title>
        <p id="Par22">The results were compared and regions with the highest score and with the maximum overlap that induce the cellular and humoral immune responses were identified and finally the selected epitopes were joined by linkers.</p>
        <sec id="Sec10">
          <title>Linkers</title>
          <p id="Par23">The final selected epitopes need a certain degree of flexibility; in this research, two flexible linkers (GSGSGS and GGGGS) were used to connect the epitopes.</p>
        </sec>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec11">
        <title>Evaluation of antigenicity and allergenicity of the novel vaccine</title>
        <p id="Par24">Recombinant vaccine was experiment for antigenicity using VaxiJen v2.0 server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/vaxijen/VaxiJen/VaxiJen.html">http://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/vaxijen/VaxiJen/VaxiJen.html</ext-link>) with a threshold value&#x2009;&#x2265;&#x2009;0.4. This server&#x2019;s approach for antigen prediction is based on auto cross covariance (ACC) transformation of protein sequences into uniform vectors with amino acid features [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR42">42</xref>]. Allertop v2.0 server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/AllerTOP/">https://www.ddg-pharmfac.net/AllerTOP/</ext-link>) with threshold value of&#x2009;&#x2212;&#x2009;0.4 was used to prevent allergic reactions to recombinant vaccine epitopes. The approach of this server is based on converting ACC of protein sequence into uniform vectors of equal length [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR43">43</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec12">
        <title>Physicochemical characterization and solubility novel vaccine</title>
        <p id="Par25">The ProtParam server) <ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://web.expasy.org/protparam/">https://web.expasy.org/protparam/</ext-link> (generates physico-chemical parameters for an amino acid sequence. The computed parameters include the grand average of hydropathicity (GRAVY), instability index, aliphatic index, extinction coefficient, atomic composition, estimated half-life, amino acid composition, theoretical pI, and molecular weight [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR44">44</xref>].</p>
        <p id="Par26">The Protein-sol server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://protein-sol.manchester.ac.uk/">https://protein-sol.manchester.ac.uk/</ext-link>) was used to analyze the solubility of the recombinant antigen. This server is a web based suite of theoretical calculations and predictive algorithms for understanding protein solubility and stability [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR45">45</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec13">
        <title>Protein secondary structure prediction</title>
        <p id="Par27">PSIPRED web server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://bioinf.cs.ucl.ac.uk/psipred/">http://bioinf.cs.ucl.ac.uk/psipred/</ext-link>) was used to predict the second structure. This server uses a very stringent cross-validation method to evaluate the performance of the method. The Q3 score averages 81.6% [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR46">46</xref>].</p>
        <p id="Par28">GOR IV server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://npsa.lyon.inserm.fr/cgi-bin/npsa_automat.pl?page=/NPSA/npsa_gor4.html">https://npsa.lyon.inserm.fr/cgi-bin/npsa_automat.pl?page=/NPSA/npsa_gor4.html</ext-link>) was used to predict secondary structure novel multi-epitopes construct [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR47">47</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec14">
        <title>Protein tertiary structure prediction</title>
        <p id="Par29">I-TASSER server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://zhanggroup.org/I-TASSER/">https://zhanggroup.org/I-TASSER/</ext-link>) was used to predict the 3D structure and function of recombinant antigen. This server uses a hierarchical method for prediction. The evaluation parameter of this server to choose the best model is based on C-score. C-score is a confidence score for estimating the quality of the predicted 3D structures; the score range of this server is (&#x2212;&#x2009;5, 2), where a C-score with a higher score indicates a higher quality model [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR48">48</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR49">49</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec15">
        <title>Refinement of the 3D modeled structure</title>
        <p id="Par30">GalaxyRefine server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://galaxy.seoklab.org/cgi-bin/submit.cgi">http://galaxy.seoklab.org/cgi-bin/submit.cgi</ext-link>) is used to refine the best 3D model of I-TASSER server. This server provides more accurate structures for determining the experimental structure, functional study, and molecular design [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR50">50</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec16">
        <title>Validation of the 3D modeled structure</title>
        <p id="Par31">Three-dimensional refined recombinant vaccine structures were validated by ProSAweb, Molprobity, and ERRAT servers. The ProSAweb server at <ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://prosa.services.came.sbg.ac.at/prosa.php">https://prosa.services.came.sbg.ac.at/prosa.php</ext-link> calculates the overall quality score by checking it out the atomic peculiarities of the model. The ProSA-web <italic>z</italic>-score is depicted on a plan, which contains the <italic>z</italic>-score of the experimental structures entrusted in PDB. This server only needs C&#x3B1; atoms to evaluate low-resolution structures [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR51">51</xref>]. Molprobity server (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="http://molprobity.biochem.duke.edu">http://molprobity.biochem.duke.edu</ext-link>) was used for the Ramachandran plot [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR52">52</xref>]. Calculate phi/psi angles (&#x3D5;, &#x3C8;) for any antigenic protein residue, and the residuals include favored regions, allowed regions, and outlier regions [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR53">53</xref>]. Also, the ERRAT tool (<ext-link ext-link-type="uri" xlink:href="https://saves.mbi.ucla.edu/">https://saves.mbi.ucla.edu/</ext-link>) was used. This tool is used to verifying protein structures determined by crystallography [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR54">54</xref>].</p>
      </sec>
      <sec id="Sec17">
        <title>Optimization and in silico cloning</title>

    <sec id="Sec32">
      <title>Discussion</title>
      <p id="Par47">Brucellosis is one of the common diseases between humans and animals, which is associated with fever in humans and abortion in animals [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR57">57</xref>]. Approximately 500,000 cases of brucellosis are reported annually worldwide. The spread of this disease is increasing due to tourist trips, trade, and immigration [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR58">58</xref>]. Lack of effective human vaccine, long treatment period, relatively low efficiency of animal vaccines, and the process of prevention and treatment of this disease have faced serious problems. Accordingly, there is an urgent need for new approaches or effective recombinant vaccines to fight against <italic>Brucella</italic> [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR59">59</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR60">60</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par48">Currently, human vaccine against this disease is not available and the vaccines that are used are live attenuated vaccines to immunize animals, but these vaccines have problems such as abortion in livestock, disease in humans, and production of antibodies that make it difficult to diagnose infected animals from vaccinated animals [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR61">61</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par49">Therefore, trying to produce an effective vaccine to prevent this infection is inevitable. Studies show that in vaccine design, the use of several different antigens in the vaccine composition can be more effective in its performance [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR62">62</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR63">63</xref>]. In this research, we used different immunoinformatics tools to design a multi-epitope recombinant construct. According to previous research, Omp31, Omp25, and Omp28 antigens were selected for use in this study, because they are highly conserved and effective in creating an immune response [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR64">64</xref>, <xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR65">65</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par50">In the first step of this study, using in silico tools, we identified the immunogenic epitopes of 3 antigens and removed the signal peptide sequences from the final antigen sequences.</p>
      <p id="Par51">Previous studies show that in order to increase the effectiveness of <italic>Brucella</italic> vaccines, they should stimulate humoral and cellular immune responses [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR66">66</xref>]. Therefore, the second step in this study is to predict the potential epitopes of B cells, MHC-I, MHC-II, and CTL, which were predicted by online servers (ABCpred, Bcepred, RANKPEP, SYFPEITHI, MHCPred, and CTLPred). Then, the epitopes with the highest score and the most overlap (thirty-one epitopes) were selected and fused together by flexible linkers (GSGSGS and GGGGS). Linkers are an important component of multi-epitope fusion antigens, increase stability in the structure of recombinant antigens, and improve biological activity and increase expression efficiency [<xref ref-type="bibr" rid="CR67">67</xref>].</p>
      <p id="Par52">The third step of this study was the examination of the immunological characteristics and the physical and chemical characteristics of the recombinant construct, which was analyzed based on immunoinformatics methods. The obtained results showed that the recombinant construct is a strong antigen and non-allergenic and had an acceptable solubility percentage. The results of physicochemical properties by ProtParam tool showed that this multi-epitope protein is classified as a stable protein.</p>
      <p id="Par53">The fourth step of this study was to investigate the secondary and tertiary structure of the recombinant vaccine. Studies on secondary structures showed 27.51% extended strand, 8.90% alpha helix, and 63.59% random coil. The results of the three-dimensional structure of the recombinant vaccine by the I-TASSER server showed that model 1 is the best model to choose among the 5 models, because model 1 has an estimated TM score of 0.54&#x2009;&#xB1;&#x2009;0.15 (TM score over 0.5 always indicates the correct model) and C-score of&#x2009;&#x2212;&#x2009;1.43 (the highest C-score among the 5 models).</p>
      <p id="Par54">The fifth step of this study was to modify the 3D model by GalaxyWEB server. And after refining the 3D structure, the model was validated by ProSA-web, Molprobity, and ERRAT servers. From the ProSA-web server, the <italic>z</italic>-score shows the overall quality of the model. Its value is displayed in the graph (<italic>z</italic>-score&#x2009;&#x2212;&#x2009;3.47), which includes the <italic>z</italic>-scores of all experimental protein chains determined in the recombinant protein structure. The Ramachandran plot showed 79.3% residues in most favored regions, 17.0% residues in additional allowed regions, 1.3% residues in generously allowed regions, and 2.4% residues in disallowed regions. The results of the Ramachandran plot show that the quality of the 3D structure is acceptable. Finally, the ERRAT tool has been used for non-bonded interactions between different types of atoms. The higher the scores in ERRAT, the higher the quality of the 3D structure. The accepted range for the model is higher than 50. The quality score of recombinant protein is 61.842%.</p>
      <p id="Par55">In the final steps of this study, we optimized our recombinant nucleotide sequence to increase the expression of multi-epitope vaccine in <italic>E. coli</italic>. The codon compatibility index (CAI) of the sequence changed from 0.5 to 1.0 and the GC content of the sequence changed from 65.21 to 51.29%.</p>
      <p id="Par56">The results of immunoinformatics analysis showed that the designed multi-epitope construct was a suitable antigen and has the ability to be tested as a vaccine candidate.</p>
    </sec>
    <sec id="Sec33">
      <title>Conclusion</title>
      <p id="Par57">In the present study, using in silico tools, a multi-epitope recombinant vaccine against <italic>B. melitensis</italic> was designed. The purpose of using this method is to save time and money as much as possible. Based on the results of immunoinformatics, the designed construction has virtuous structural, suitable physiochemical characteristics, high antigenicity, good stability, and appropriate solubility, as well as non-allergenic. Therefore, this recombinant vaccine can be used as a suitable candidate to fight against brucellosis infection. But in order to confirm the results of the present studies, the analysis of immunogenicity in in vitro and in vivo tests is very necessary.</p>
    </sec>
