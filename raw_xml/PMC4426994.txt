xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" content-type="pmc-pdf" xlink:href="nihms413557.pdf"/><abstract><p id="P1">Brucellosis is the most common zoonotic disease worldwide and there is no vaccine for human use. <italic toggle="yes">Brucella melitensis</italic> Rev1, a live attenuated strain, is the commercial vaccine for small ruminants to prevent <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic> infections but has been associated with abortions in animals. Moreover, strain Rev1 is known to cause disease in humans and cannot be used for human vaccination. Outer membrane vesicles (OMVs) obtained from <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic> have been shown to provide protection similar to strain Rev1 in mice against <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic> challenge. In the present work we tested the efficacy of Pluronic P85 as an adjuvant to enhance the efficacy of <italic toggle="yes">Brucella</italic> OMVs as a vaccine. P85 enhanced the <italic toggle="yes">in vitro</italic> secretion of TNF-&#945; by macrophages induced with OMVs and P85. Further, P85 enhanced the protection provided by OMVs against <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic> challenge. This enhanced protection was associated with higher total IgG antibody production but not increased IFN-&#947; or IL-4 cytokine levels. Moreover, P85 alone provided significantly better clearance of <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic> compared to saline vaccinated mice. Further studies are warranted to find the mechanism of action of P85 that provides non-specific protection and enhances the efficacy of OMVs as a vaccine against <italic toggle="yes">B. melitensis</italic>.</p></abstract><kwd-group><kwd>Outer membrane vesicles</kwd><kwd>Pluronics</kwd><kwd><italic toggle="yes">Brucella
